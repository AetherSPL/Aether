Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2025 · Last Updated: March 2025

AetherScan is designed with privacy in mind. We do not sell your data, do not require registration, and do not store personally identifiable information beyond what is technically necessary to operate the Service.

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how AetherScan ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and handles information in connection with your use of the AetherScan blockchain explorer at aetherscan.io (the "Service").

AetherScan is a read-layer blockchain analytics tool. By design, it does not require account creation, identity verification, or the submission of personal data to access core functionality. The blockchain data displayed on AetherScan is public information derived from the Solana-compatible ledger.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

The Service collects minimal information directly from users:

  • Wallet addresses: When you search for or submit a wallet address (e.g., to use the faucet or look up a balance), that address is processed to retrieve publicly available blockchain data. Wallet addresses are not stored permanently beyond the immediate request.
  • Faucet requests: To prevent abuse of the token faucet, we record the wallet address of each faucet claim and the timestamp of the claim. This data is stored on the server's persistent disk and is used solely for rate-limiting purposes.
  • AI chat messages: If you use the Aether AI feature, your message is transmitted to Anthropic's Claude API to generate a response. We do not permanently store AI conversation history. Anthropic's data handling is governed by Anthropic's Privacy Policy.

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you access the Service, our server automatically collects:

IP addressServer security, abuse preventionStandard server logs, up to 30 days

2.3 Blockchain Data

All blockchain data displayed on AetherScan — including transaction histories, wallet balances, block contents, and token information — is public data derived from an immutable distributed ledger. AetherScan does not create this data; it indexes and presents publicly available on-chain records. This data cannot be deleted, modified, or removed by AetherScan or by request.

3. Cookies and Local Storage

AetherScan uses a single session cookie to maintain your authenticated state during a browsing session. This cookie:

  • Contains only a random session token — no personal information
  • Is not used for tracking or advertising
  • Expires when the server restarts or you clear your browser cookies
  • Is required for the Service to function (authentication)

We do not use third-party tracking cookies, advertising cookies, or analytics cookies.

4. How We Use Information

We use the limited information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Operating and maintaining the Service
  • Preventing abuse of the token faucet
  • Detecting and preventing fraudulent or malicious activity
  • Diagnosing and resolving technical issues
  • Responding to support inquiries

We do not use your information for advertising, marketing profiling, or sale to third parties.

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your information. We may share limited information only in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers: We use Anthropic's API to power Aether AI. Messages you send to Aether AI are processed by Anthropic subject to their privacy policy.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required to do so by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.
  • Protection of rights: We may disclose information where we believe it is necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of AetherScan, our users, or the public.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, user information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

6. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the limited information we process. This includes encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS), secure server infrastructure, and access controls. However, no system is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Because AetherScan does not store sensitive personal information (such as private keys, payment data, or identity documents), the risk profile of our data handling is inherently limited.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights regarding your personal data, including:

  • Access: The right to know what data we hold about you
  • Deletion: The right to request deletion of data we hold (noting that blockchain data is immutable and cannot be deleted)
  • Correction: The right to correct inaccurate information
  • Portability: The right to receive data in a portable format
  • Objection: The right to object to certain uses of your data

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@aetherscan.io. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed at children under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected information from a child, we will take steps to delete it promptly.

9. International Data Transfers

AetherScan operates globally. If you access the Service from outside the country in which our servers are located, your information may be transferred across international borders. By using the Service, you consent to such transfers, which will be conducted in accordance with applicable law.

10. Third-Party Links

The Service may contain links to third-party websites, including blockchain explorers, token data providers, and social media platforms. This Privacy Policy does not apply to third-party sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you visit.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of material changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Your continued use of the Service after any modification constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
